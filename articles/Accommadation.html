Accomadation-Advice

By Dáithí O'Connell

Throughout my travels I encountered my fair share of difficulties regarding Accommodation but thankfully I found a bed for myself every night.

Hostels

If you’re on a budget holiday Hostelworld is the app for you. You can sort the app by a basis of cheapest first to get the best price and I can’t recommend the review section enough. However, some destinations have very expensive hostels (e.g. Amsterdam, Dubrovnik), form my experience around Europe I found that €15 per night is expensive. I am aware that when ‘winging it’ it can be difficult to gauge your whereabouts in the near future.

Travel Tip: to avoid absurd prices is to book well in advance, hostels are priced by supply and demand so even 4 days in advance can half the price you may pay on that day.

BNB's

Sometimes it’s good to shop around for substitutes such as BNB’s and hotels. I can’t mention BNB’s without mentioning Airbnb. If there’s 4 or over of you I highly recommend Airbnb. When I was in Dubrovnik hostels were priced at €25 each. From a quick search on Airbnb we found a beautiful apartment within the old town walls for around €15 each for between the 5 of us. Although hostels are much more fun for meeting other travellers your sleep is easily disrupted. Airbnb is often the more comfortable option.

Hotels

During peak times and events sometimes hostels can book up or prices can increase substantially. Sometimes, even for budget travellers getting last minute deals on hotel rooms can be more economical. Similarly, with Airbnb this can obviously work out cheaper if 3 or 4 are willing to share a large room. Hotels.com website and app does the job perfect when booking hotels, with over 325,00 hotels and an easy to use navigation.
